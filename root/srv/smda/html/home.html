Welcome to the SMDA web viewer! Click on Atlas to begin viewing. Here is some information about the current modalities available:

Accessory Overlays – processed contrasts that are often visualized overlaid on anatomical or histological data

  • DTI glyphs – glyphs, or visualizations, of the apparent diffusion constant (ADC) profile in each voxel estimated by the diffusion tensor
  • CSD glyphs – glyphs, or visualizations, of the fiber orientation distribution (FOD) estimated using constrained spherical deconvolution
  • ROI Label – anatomical labels for white matter (WM) and gray matter (GM) regions created using cytoarchitectural features from Nissl Stains and diffusion white matter fiber tractography
  • BDA Count Label – a “count” of the number of BDA labelled fibers in each voxel, determined automatically through image processing

 

MRI – contrasts obtained directly from scanner or through processed MRI images

  • T2 – T2 contrast obtained using either gradient echo multi-slice sequences or 3d gradient echo sequences
  • Diffusion Tensor – 6 components of the estimated diffusion tensor
  • FA – fractional anisotropy from the diffusion tensor, a scalar value between zero and one that describes the degree of anisotropy of diffusion
  • MD – the mean, or average, diffusivity in a voxel
  • Mean B0 – the mean, or average, of all non-diffusion weighted images
  • Mean DW - the mean, or average, of all diffusion weighted images
  • RA – relative anisotropy, or volume ratio of the diffusion tensor
  • VEC1 – primary eigenvector of the diffusion tensor (3 components)
  • VR – volume ratio of the diffusion tensor
  • CMAP – colormap, where red, green, and blue represent primary eigenvectors in the right/left, anterior/posterior, and superior/inferior directions, respectively

 

Block – frozen tissue block

  • Block – photographs of frozen tissue block as it is being sectioned

 

Histology - examination of the microscopic anatomy of the tissue using light or confocal microscopes

  • BDA – Biotinylated Dextran Amine, a tracer injected into the left hemisphere primary motor cortex (M1) used for studying neural pathways
  • Nissl – Cresyl Violet Nissl stain for identification of cell bodies
  • Myelin – Gallyas silver stain to identify myelinated axons
  • DiI (coming soon) – Lipophilic fluorescent dye that is used to label myelinated axons and imaged using 3D confocal microscopy.
